Recent findings suggest that transformer-based language models demonstrate emergent capabilities at scale (Wei et al., 2022). Specifically, models with over 100 billion parameters show sudden improvements in multi-step reasoning tasks that are not observed in smaller models. A study by Hoffmann et al. (2022) found that compute-optimal training requires roughly 20 tokens per parameter, challenging previous scaling assumptions. Furthermore, Brown et al. (2020) demonstrated that GPT-3 achieves few-shot performance competitive with fine-tuned models on several NLP benchmarks, with accuracy reaching 71.2% on the SuperGLUE benchmark without any gradient updates.
