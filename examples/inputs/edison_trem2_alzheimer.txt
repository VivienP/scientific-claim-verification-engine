Question: What is the role of TREM2 in Alzheimer's microglia, and what are the key quantitative findings?

## Technical summary: role of TREM2 in Alzheimer’s microglia

### Functional role in microglial biology during AD
Triggering receptor expressed on myeloid cells 2 (TREM2) is a microglia-enriched immunoreceptor that signals via the adaptor DAP12/TYROBP and is repeatedly implicated in the transition from a homeostatic program toward plaque-associated disease-responsive programs (often described as DAM/ARM/MGnD-like states), enabling microglia to proliferate/survive, migrate/chemotax toward pathology, and execute lipid- and debris-handling functions (ulrich2017elucidatingtherole pages 5-6, mcquade2020geneexpressionand pages 1-2, kerenshaul2017auniquemicroglia pages 8-10). In mouse AD models, loss or reduction of TREM2 consistently reduces plaque-associated microglial clustering and microgliosis; Trem2 deficiency is also associated with increased plaque-associated neuritic/axonal dystrophy, consistent with a microglial “barrier/compaction” protective function near plaques (ulrich2017elucidatingtherole pages 5-6, yeh2017trem2microgliaand pages 15-17).

Mechanistically, TREM2 functions as a sensor of lipids and pathological aggregates. A key quantitative demonstration is direct binding of oligomeric amyloid-β (Aβ) to TREM2 and downstream activation of SYK/AKT signaling, providing a plausible ligand-driven route for microglia to detect Aβ-rich environments and mount a response (zhong2018amyloidbetamodulatesmicroglial pages 4-6, zhong2018amyloidbetamodulatesmicroglial media 0f3d900d).

In human AD tissue, microglial subpopulation composition is strongly shaped by Aβ/tau pathology and by APOE/TREM2 genotypes. A prominent example is the CD163+ “amyloid-responsive microglia” (ARM) state, which is depleted in carriers of the TREM2 R47H risk variant, consistent with impaired plaque-adaptive microglial programming in risk-variant carriers (nguyen2020apoeandtrem2 pages 13-14). Complementing these tissue observations, human iPSC-derived microglia lacking TREM2 display reduced survival signaling, impaired chemotaxis via SDF-1α/CXCR4, and impaired uptake of key substrates such as lipidated APOE, consistent with loss of core microglial “sensome” and response functions relevant to AD lesions (mcquade2020geneexpressionand pages 4-5, mcquade2020geneexpressionand pages 7-8).

#### Highlights (numbers that most directly anchor the microglial role)
* **Direct Aβ sensing:** oligomeric Aβ1–42 binds sTREM2-Fc with **KD = 86.50 nM**, supporting high-affinity recognition of a disease-relevant Aβ species (zhong2018amyloidbetamodulatesmicroglial pages 4-6, zhong2018amyloidbetamodulatesmicroglial media 0f3d900d).
* **Human microglial state composition depends on TREM2 genotype:** ARM constituted **77.5%** of microglia in **A+T−** vs **7.8%** in **A−T−** brains (**OR 40.53**), while **TREM2 R47H** cases had **15.0% ARM** (**OR 0.509** vs TREM2-WT A+T+), indicating depletion of a plaque-responsive state in risk-variant carriers (nguyen2020apoeandtrem2 pages 13-14).
* **Human microglial chemotaxis signaling:** in iPSC microglia, **39%** of WT vs **11.5%** of TREM2 KO cells responded to SDF-1α, quantifying impaired CXCR4-linked chemotactic signaling (mcquade2020geneexpressionand pages 7-8).
* **Neuroprotective barrier association:** dystrophic axon formation around plaques was **70% less prevalent** in areas “protected by microglia,” consistent with plaque-proximal microglial shielding/compaction functions that are disrupted when TREM2-dependent plaque responses are impaired (yeh2017trem2microgliaand pages 15-17).

### Notes on what remains quantitatively under-specified in retrieved excerpts
